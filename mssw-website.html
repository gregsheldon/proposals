2. Positioning & Messaging Strategy

MSSW will be positioned as a company that helps clients reduce risk and protect margins. The website should communicate that MSSW brings experience, technical capability, and reliable execution to commercial fabrication and installation work. That means shifting the copy from “what we do” to “why clients trust us to get it done right."

"Custom metal fabrication and installation for commercial projects that demand precision, compliance, and dependable turnaround."

Core Positioning Themes

Delivering on time and exactly as specified to protect the schedule and avoid costly delays.

Code-Conscious Fabrication

Providing clear, fast answers to keep projects moving and protect your budget from scope confusion.

Delivering practical install planning, field measuring, and seamless jobsite execution on commercial schedules.

3. Conversion-First Design

Before scaling traffic, the site must be redesigned to capture it efficiently. Commercial buyers move quickly, so the design should feature a clean, grid-based layout with modern typography and generous spacing to reduce friction and make the next step obvious.

Inside the Mind of a Commercial Buyer

Instead of generic “about us” language, the website will speak directly to the concerns of general contractors, estimators, and facility managers: missed deadlines, budget overruns, failed inspections, and coordination headaches. We will replace friction with trust signals like specific process photos, organized capability highlights, and highly functional contact forms.

Top Conversion Priorities:

  • Prominent "Request a Quote" buttons anchored on every core page.
  • Dedicated Upload Portal for drawings and BOMs tailored for estimators and contractors.
  • Frictionless contact forms for rapid project inquiries.
  • Click-to-call functionality optimized for mobile users on job sites.
  • Visual proof through featured projects, process photos, and capability highlights.
